In the FinArg dataset, an argument is a micro-structure unit in the answers of company representatives during the Q&A sections of earnings conference calls, where the speaker attempts to justify an opinion or convince analysts of a standpoint. An argument consists of a single claim, defined as the root of the argument, supported or attacked by one or more premises that provide reasons or evidence for that claim. Argumentative text is identified at the sentence or clause level when there is an explicit inference relation between components, such as a premise leading to a claim. Text that provides general information or statements without participating in such an inference relation is annotated as non-argumentative. The dataset focuses exclusively on arguments as independent units without modeling higher-level relations between different arguments.
