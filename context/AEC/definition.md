In this dataset, an argument is a sentence from online debate dialog that begins with one of the discourse connectives "But", "If", "So", or "First". These sentence-initial markers were identified as reliable indicators of argumentative content based on the Implicit Markup Hypothesis, which posits that speakers mark argumentative structure through their choice of discourse connectives: "But" signals contrast or challenge to an opponent's claim, "If" signals contingency or conditional reasoning, "So" signals consequence or conclusion, and "First" signals specification of a detailed argument. A sentence is classified as non-argumentative if it does not begin with any of these four discourse markers.
