In this dataset, an argument consists of argumentative components that express claims or provide evidential support within scientific publications. Claims are defined as argumentative statements and are divided into Own Claims, which closely relate to the authors’ own work, and Background Claims, which relate to the background of the work, such as common knowledge, related work, or established practices in the research field. Data components are facts that serve as evidence for or against a claim, including references or factual information used to support or contradict claims. These components may be connected by argumentative relations such as support or contradiction, and their spans are not restricted to sentence boundaries but may range from a single token to multiple sentences. The paper does not explicitly define categories of non-argumentative text beyond the absence of these argumentative components.
