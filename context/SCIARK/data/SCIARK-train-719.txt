In 2015, the lowest county-level maternal mortality ratio was 3·4 per 100 000 livebirths in Nanhu District, Zhejiang Province.
The highest was still in Zanda County, Tibet, but the fall to 830·5 per 100 000 livebirths was only 76·3%.
26 ethnic groups had population majorities in at least one county in China, and all had achieved declines in maternal mortality ratios in line with the pace of MDG 5.
