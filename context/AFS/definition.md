In this dataset, an argument is a single sentence from online debate dialog that clearly expresses a specific, recognizable point about a debate topic (such as gun control, death penalty, or gay marriage). The sentence must be understandable on its own without requiring surrounding context and must convey a clear position, claim, or premise that supports or attacks a stance on a specific aspect of the debate. Examples of arguments include statements like "Gun control laws do not stop criminals from getting guns" or "The death penalty deters crime" because they express clear, specific points about recognizable debate aspects. Non-argumentative sentences include vague reactions, sarcastic remarks, personal anecdotes, or statements that are clearly written but do not contribute to a specific debate point. Importantly, a sentence can be clearly interpretable yet still be non-argumentative if it does not relate to a recognizable aspect of the debate topic.
