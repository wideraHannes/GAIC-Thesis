In this dataset, an argument is a sentential argument: a single sentence from dialog that clearly expresses an argument facet, including an argument itself or a premise or conclusion that can be used in an argument about a facet or sub-issue of the debate topic. Such sentences must be easily interpretable on their own, without requiring surrounding context, and convey propositional content relevant to supporting or attacking a position. Argumentative sentences are identified as those that clearly express an argument rather than vague reactions or uninterpretable text. Sentences that are clearly interpretable but do not seem to be part of an argument about a facet or sub-issue are not considered arguments, nor are sentences that cannot be interpreted as an argument at all.
