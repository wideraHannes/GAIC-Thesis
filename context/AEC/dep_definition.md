In this dataset, an argument is a sentence from online debate dialog that clearly expresses a specific claim or position that can be understood without additional context. The sentence must be self-contained and convey a clear stance, justification, or challenge on a debate topic. Examples of arguments include "But guns were made specifically to kill people" or "If you ban guns crime rates will not decrease" because they express clear, understandable positions. Non-argumentative sentences include vague reactions ("Sorry but you fail again"), insults, sarcastic remarks, or statements that require significant context to understand what point is being made. A sentence is also non-argumentative if its meaning or intended argument cannot be reconstructed without knowing what came before it in the conversation.
