An argument is a context-dependent claim: a general, concise statement that directly takes a position on a given topic by either supporting it or contesting it. Such a claim must express a clear stance toward the topic rather than merely mentioning it, and it is evaluated in relation to that specific topic and its surrounding context. An argument may be accompanied by evidence—sentences that directly support the claim through facts, examples, statistics, or authoritative testimony—and in some cases the same sentence can function as both a claim and its own evidence. Claims can exist even if no supporting evidence is found nearby. What does not count as an argument includes sentences that are purely descriptive, background information, definitions, narratives, or factual statements that do not explicitly support or oppose the topic. Statements that are relevant to the topic but do not assert a clear position, as well as evidence-like sentences that only provide support without an associated claim, are also not considered arguments.
