Recalling that the total penalty of an embedding v is Γ T b(v), we can think of the maximum margin Γ as the one that best distinguishes between the best “bad” embedding and the worst “good” embedding in the training set.
In our case, however, we do not need to classify embeddings, but rather find a Γ such that the embedding with the lowest penalty f (v) = Γ T b(v) is likely to be good.
