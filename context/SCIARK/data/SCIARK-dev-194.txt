Differences in the health transition patterns of migrants and non-migrants aged 50 and older in southern and western Europe (2004-2015)
Background Most previous research on migrant health in Europe has taken a cross-sectional perspective, without a specific focus on the older population.
Having knowledge about inequalities in health transitions over the life course between migrants and non-migrants, including at older ages, is crucial for the tailoring of policies to the demands of an ageing and culturally diverse society.
