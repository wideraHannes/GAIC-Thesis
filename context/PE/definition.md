An argument is a structured expression of reasoning that aims to support or oppose a controversial standpoint by providing justifications or refutations. It consists of a central claim, which is an opinionated or debatable statement, and one or more premises, which give reasons that support or attack that claim or another premise. Argumentative components are explicitly linked by directed relations indicating support or attack, forming a coherent reasoning structure rather than isolated statements. Claims can stand as the focal point of an argument, while premises function as evidence or reasoning and typically have an outgoing relation to another component. Text is not considered an argument if it merely introduces a topic, provides background information, describes facts without a justificatory intent, summarizes content, or expresses opinions without giving or implying reasons. Likewise, narrative, descriptive, or rhetorical text that does not participate in a support or attack relation is non-argumentative.
