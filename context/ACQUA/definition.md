In this dataset, an argument is a sentence that contains a comparison between at least two target items and expresses a clear preference direction, indicating that one item is better or worse than the other. Such sentences are annotated as BETTER when the first item is favored or WORSE when the first item is disfavored, and they are treated as pro or con arguments for the compared items. Comparative sentences are only considered argumentative if they have an obvious “winner” of the comparison. Sentences that do not contain a comparison between the target items, that merely list items, or that pose comparative questions are annotated as NONE and are not considered arguments.
