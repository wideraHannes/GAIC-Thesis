Despite this, most character animation in interactive applications, such as video games, is based on a geometric skeletal deformation technique commonly referred to as linear blending, or matrix palette skinning, or Skeletal-Subspace Deformation (SSD), in which vertex locations are weighted averages of points in several coordinate frames (see 
[Magnenat-Thalmann et al. 1988; Magnenat-Thalmann and Thalmann 1991]).
