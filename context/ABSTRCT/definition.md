In the ABSTRCT dataset, an argument is a text span in a randomized controlled trial abstract that plays a role in evidence-based reasoning about the study’s outcome. Arguments are annotated as either claims or evidence. A claim is a concluding statement by the authors about the effects or implications of the intervention, typically expressing how a treatment compares to a control and derived from the study results; more general or overarching conclusions (major claims) are treated as claims. Evidence consists of concrete observations or measurements reported in the trial, such as outcome values, statistical results, or side effects, which can support or undermine a claim or another piece of evidence. These components need not correspond to full sentences and can appear anywhere in the abstract, including introductory or concluding positions. Text that merely describes background information, study design details, procedural steps, or general domain knowledge without making or supporting a conclusion about the study outcome is not considered an argument. Likewise, neutral descriptions that do not function as observations supporting or attacking a claim are labeled as non-argumentative.
