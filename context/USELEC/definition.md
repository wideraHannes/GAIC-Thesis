In the USElecDeb60To16 dataset, an argument is any text span annotated as an argument component, namely a claim or a premise. Claims are the ultimate goals of an argument and include policies advocated by a party or candidate, judgments about other candidates or parties, or stances and opinions toward controversial subjects that require justification to be accepted by the audience. Premises are assertions provided to support claims, functioning as reasons or justifications, and may take the form of past experience, statistics, examples, or other evidence. A sentence is considered argumentative if it contains at least one such argument component. Sentences that contain no claims or premises are treated as non-argumentative.
