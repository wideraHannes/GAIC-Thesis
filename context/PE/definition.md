In this dataset, an argument is defined as a structured set of argument components within persuasive essays that aim to increase or decrease the acceptability of a controversial standpoint. Argumentative content consists of statements that function as a major claim, claim, or premise: the major claim represents the author’s overall standpoint, a claim is a central controversial statement within an individual argument, and premises are reasons that justify or refute a claim. Argument components are connected by directed argumentative relations of support or attack, where a source component provides a justification for or a refutation of a target component. An argument minimally includes a claim supported by at least one premise, and more complex arguments may include serial chains of premises. The paper explicitly distinguishes non-argumentative text as text units that do not contain argumentative content, such as introductory topic descriptions or other sentences without claims or premises.
