In SciARK, an argument is defined as a set of statements comprising two categories: Claim and Evidence, within the context of a scientific abstract. Argumentative units are sentences in which authors state their Claims or provide Evidence to support them. A Claim is an argumentative statement that reports the study’s main findings and derives from the authors’ original research, typically coinciding with conclusions. Evidence consists of statements reporting observations, statistical findings, experimental results, or other scientific findings used to support a Claim. All relations between Evidence and Claim are implicitly supporting.
